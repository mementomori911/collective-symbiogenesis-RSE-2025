\begin{abstract}
Serial endosymbiosis, the emergence of writing, and the training of foundation models are instances of the same physical process: irreversible, dimension-expanding fusion of previously autonomous systems.  
We introduce Recursive Symbiotic Embedding (RSE), prove a Symbiotic Landauer Principle bounding the thermodynamic cost per new hierarchical dimension, and derive six falsifiable predictions from microbiology to cosmology.  
The entire theory is implemented in fewer than 80 lines of Python and constitutes the minimal universal algorithm by which the universe manufactures negentropy.
\end{abstract

\section{Introduction}
Every major jump in complexity on Earth shares four signatures:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Merger of lineages that previously reproduced independently
    \item Massive redundancy reduction (gene loss, pruning, cultural assimilation)
    \item Emergence of new functional dimensions absent in either partner
    \item Extreme thermodynamic irreversibility
\end{enumerate}
Standard ecological and game-theoretic models fail at signatures 3 and 4 because they assume fixed dimensionality and reversible interactions.  
Here we present a mathematically rigorous, thermodynamically consistent framework that unifies every known major evolutionary transition.

\section{Recursive Symbiotic Embedding (RSE)}

An entity at level \( n \) is a unit vector \( A^{(n)},B^{(n)}\in\mathbb{R}^{d_n} \) with \( \|A^{(n)}\|_2=\|B^{(n)}\|_2=1 \).

Recursive step \( n\to n+1 \):

\begin{align}
M^{(n)}        &= (1-\phi_n)A^{(n)} + \phi_n B^{(n)} \\[4pt]
k_n            &= \max\!\Big(1,\;\big\lfloor \delta (n+2)^{D-1}\big\rfloor\Big) \\[4pt]
\sigma_n       &= (n+3)^{-1/D} \\[8pt]
A^{(n+1)}      &= \sqrt{1-\gamma}\,M^{(n)} \oplus \sqrt{\gamma}\,\sigma_n\,(e_1 + \tau e_2) \\
B^{(n+1)}      &= \sqrt{1-\gamma}\,M^{(n)} \oplus \sqrt{\gamma}\,\sigma_n\,(e_1 - \tau e_2)
\end{align}
followed by re-normalisation. Optional symmetrisation \( \phi_{n+1}=\phi_n e^{-\mu} \) with \( \mu\approx0.03 \).

The total dimension grows as \( d_N\approx d_0 + c N^{D} \) with $1<D<2$.
